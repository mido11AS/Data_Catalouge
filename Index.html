We historically referenced heights to mean sea level, and many believe we still do, but this is no longer true for most spatial data analyses. Because we have two reference surfaces, a geiod and an ellipsoid, we also have two bases from which to measure height. Elevation is typically defined as the distance above a geoid. This height above a geoid is also called the orthometric height (Figure 3- 9). Heights above an ellipsoid are often referred to as ellipsoidal height. These are illustrated in Figure 3-9, with the ellipsoidalheight labeled h, and orthometric height labeled H. The difference between the ellipsoidalheight and geoidal height at any location,shown in Figure 3-9 as N, has various names, including geoidal height and geoidal separation.
